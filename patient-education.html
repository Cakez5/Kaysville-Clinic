Cortisone Injections
Cortisone injections are a type of steroid medication that is injected into
the body to reduce inflammation and pain. They contain a synthetic
version of cortisol, which is a hormone naturally produced by the body.
These injections are commonly used to treat conditions such as arthritis,
tendonitis, bursitis, and other inflammatory disorders.

What can Cortisone Injections Treat?
• Arthritis: Cortisone injections can provide relief for patients with
arthritis, including osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis.
• Tendonitis: These injections can help reduce pain and inflammation
in the tendons, which are tissues that connect muscles to bones.
• Bursitis: Bursae are small fluid-filled sacs that cushion and lubricate
joints. When these sacs become inflamed, cortisone injections can
be used to reduce pain and inflammation.

Benefits of Cortisone Injections
• Quick relief: Cortisone injections provide fast and effective pain relief
for a variety of conditions.
• Minimally invasive: These injections are administered directly into the
affected area, making them less invasive compared to other
treatment options.
• Long-lasting effects: The effects of cortisone injections can last for
several weeks or months, providing extended relief from pain and
inflammation.

Hyalgan and Synvisc Injections
Hyalgan and Synvisc are types of hyaluronic acid injections. Hyaluronic
acid is a substance naturally found in the body that helps lubricate and
cushion joints. These injections are used to treat osteoarthritis, a
degenerative joint disease that causes pain and stiffness in the joints.

What can Hyalgan and Synvisc Injections Treat?
• Osteoarthritis: These injections can provide relief for patients with
mild to moderate osteoarthritis by reducing pain and improving joint
mobility.

Benefits of Hyalgan and Synvisc Injections
• Lubrication: By injecting hyaluronic acid into the joints, these
injections provide lubrication to help reduce pain and improve joint
movement.
• Long-lasting effects: The benefits of these injections can last for
several months, providing extended relief from osteoarthritis
symptoms.

B12 Injections
B12 injections are a type of vitamin supplement that contains high levels of
vitamin B12. This essential nutrient is important for various bodily functions,
including the production of red blood cells and maintaining a healthy
nervous system.

What can B12 Injections Treat?
• Vitamin deficiency: These injections can help treat vitamin B12
deficiency, which can lead to symptoms such as fatigue, weakness,
and numbness in the hands and feet.

Benefits of B12 Injections
• Boosts energy: By increasing vitamin B12 levels in the body, these

Erectile Dysfunction in Men: A Comprehensive
Guide to Understanding and Treatment
Erectile dysfunction (ED) is one of the most well-known symptoms of
andropause, which is the male version of menopause. Andropause is a
condition commonly associated with the natural male aging process, and
it is often the result of low testosterone levels. Unfortunately, ED is also quite
common. In fact, some researchers estimate that as much as 40 percent
of men at the age of 40 and 70 percent of men at the age of 70 suffer from
some degree of ED.
The most prominent result of ED is, understandably, its effect on male
sexual health. However, ED can combine with other symptoms of
andropause like stress, anxiety, and reduced sex drive to form a negative
feedback loop wherein these symptoms feed off of and further contribute
to one another.
The Facts About Erectile Dysfunction
Erectile dysfunction is best described as the inability to get and keep an
erection to the point that the ability to have sex is reduced or not possible
at all. Erections are the result of sexual arousal, but they also rely on a
variety of components in the human body, including hormones, nerves,
blood vessels, and muscles.
What are the Primary Causes of Erectile
Dysfunction?

With healthy erections depending on so many different components of the
body, there are a wide range of potential sources of ED, including:
 Low testosterone levels
 Excess body fat
 Sleep issues
 Stress
 Anxiety and depression
 High blood pressure, high cholesterol, and other aspects of
cardiovascular disease
 Diabetes
 Excess use of alcohol, drugs, and tobacco
 Certain medical treatments and surgeries
 Injuries to the arteries or nerves
The Relationship Between Low Testosterone
and Erectile Dysfunction
Low testosterone levels are the primary characteristic of a condition known
as hypogonadism. Andropause, which is caused by the low levels of
testosterone associated with the natural male aging process, is also
referred to as late-onset hypogonadism (LOH). The low levels of
testosterone associated with andropause can lead to ED and a wide range
of other symptoms, many of which can also contribute to ED, including:
 Erectile dysfunction (ED)
 Reduced sex drive
 Excess body fat
 Sleep issues
 Hot flashes
 Fatigue
 Depressed mood and increased risk of depression
 Enlarged male breast tissue (gynecomastia)
 Issues with memory and focus
 Reduced muscle mass and strength

The Interplay Between Erectile Dysfunction,
Low Testosterone, and Reduced Sex Drive
Low sex drive, also known as low libido, is another one of the more
common symptoms of andropause. Some estimates state that as many
as one in five men experience low sex drive at some point in their lives. As
is the case with ED, low sex drive can be caused by a wide range of
potential sources. Some of those sources can be external, such as certain
medications and the excess use of alcohol, drugs, and tobacco.
Unfortunately, there are also a variety of potential internal sources that
often accompany andropause. These sources can also contribute to one
another in negative feedback loop relationships. For example, ED can lead
to reduced sex drive and vice versa. The same can also be said of anxiety,
depression, and stress as they relate to sex drive and ED. The range of
potential sources include:
 Hormonal imbalances, especially low testosterone
 Erectile dysfunction (ED)
 Anxiety
 Depression
 Stress
 Fatigue
 Excess body fat
 Cardiovascular disease
 Diabetes
 High blood pressure (hypertension)
What Are the Best Ways to Address Erectile
Dysfunction and Low Sex Drive?
Fortunately, there are multiple options for men to naturally address ED and
support healthy sexual function.
Stress Reduction Techniques

Stress is a major source of ED, and these two conditions often contribute to
one another. The human body naturally responds to stress with cortisol,
which is also referred to as the “stress hormone.” While cortisol allows the
body to effectively respond to stress by helping the body return to a
balanced state after exposure to stressful situations, extended and
repeated periods of stress can lead to the body producing too much
cortisol. Chronic stress and high cortisol can then contribute to one
another in a negative feedback loop and lead to a wide range of
symptoms and health conditions, including ED. Reducing stress can,
therefore, potentially address ED and contribute to healthy sexual function.
Some of the best ways to reduce stress include avoiding caffeine, eating
healthy food, practicing deep breathing, adhering to a consistent exercise
routine, and getting quality sleep on a regular basis.
Support Balanced Testosterone Levels
Since hypogonadism is such a common source of ED, it is possible for men
to address ED and support optimal sexual health by balancing levels of
testosterone.
As is the case with cortisol, it is possible to naturally support balanced
levels of testosterone by exercising regularly and adhering to a healthy
diet. Unfortunately, those methods often become less effective after the
onset of andropause.
An increasing number of men struggling with the low testosterone levels
associated with andropause are turning to hormone replacement therapy
(HRT) to promote hormonal balance. While HRT can be administered using
creams, gels, capsules, and patches, testosterone is most commonly
administered in the form of liquid injections. These weekly injections deliver
prescribed amounts of testosterone to the body directly through the
bloodstream. However, self-injecting testosterone on a weekly basis can
be inconvenient and has the potential to lead to pain and discomfort at
the injection site.

For a more convenient mode of administering testosterone medications, a
growing number of men are taking advantage of hormone pellet therapy.
Standard testosterone pellets have been developed to provide the body
with consistent amounts of testosterone for months at a time so that fewer
procedures are necessary. During a pellet insertion procedure, clusters of
prescribed hormone medications are packed into pellets and carefully
inserted under the skin. While this method has enhanced the convenience
of testosterone replacement therapy, some patients can still experience
pain and discomfort at the insertion site.

Minor Surgery

Comfortable, professional treatment for common minor procedures and outpatient care.

Our skin is the largest organ in our body, and it is exposed to various external factors on a daily basis. This can result in various skin conditions that may require minor surgical intervention to treat and improve the overall health of our skin. At Kaysville Clinic, we offer a range of minor skin procedures such as:

Skin biopsies: This procedure involves removing a small sample of skin for examination under a microscope. It is commonly used to diagnose skin conditions such as skin cancer, rashes, and infections.

Mole removal: Mole removal: Moles are usually harmless, but they may become a cosmetic concern or pose a risk of developing into skin cancer. We offer safe and effective mole removal procedures to improve the appearance of your skin and prevent potential health risks.

Wart removal: Warts are small growths on the skin caused by a virus. They can be unsightly and may cause discomfort, especially if they are located on the feet or hands. Our physicians can safely remove warts using various techniques such as freezing, burning, or cutting

Cyst removal: Cysts are abnormal sacs filled with fluid or semi-solid material that can occur under the skin, in organs, or within tissues. They can be caused by infections, blockages in ducts, or genetic conditions. During cyst removal, the cyst is carefully excised and any remaining fragments are removed to prevent regrowth. Cyst removal may be recommended if the cyst is causing discomfort, becoming infected, or if it is potentially cancerous.

Lipoma removal: Lipoma removal is a similar surgical procedure to cyst removal, but specifically targets the removal of lipomas. Lipomas are slow-growing, fatty tumors that can occur anywhere on the body where fat tissue is present. They are usually harmless and painless, but may cause discomfort if they grow large enough.

Ingrown toenails occur when the edge of a toenail grows into the surrounding skin, resulting in pain, swelling, and infection. This condition is commonly caused by incorrect trimming of nails, ill-fitting shoes, or injury. At Kaysville Clinic, we offer minor surgical procedures to treat ingrown toenails and prevent further complications. These procedures may include:

Partial nail avulsion: This procedure involves removing a small portion of the nail that is causing the ingrown toenail. The area is then treated to prevent regrowth of the nail.

Total nail avulsion: In more severe cases, the entire nail may need to be removed. This can provide immediate relief from pain and allow for proper healing of the affected area.

Matrixectomy: This procedure involves removing a portion of the nail bed to prevent the ingrown toenail from recurring.

At Kaysville Clinic, we understand that injuries can happen to anyone at any time. That's why we offer a wide range of services for the treatment of common injuries such as strains and sprains, fractures, and lacerations.

Strains and Sprains: A strain occurs when a muscle or tendon is stretched or torn, while a sprain refers to an injury to a ligament. These injuries can occur due to overuse, sudden movements, or trauma. Symptoms of strains and sprains may include pain, swelling, bruising, and difficulty moving the affected area.

Treatment: The treatment for strains and sprains may vary depending on the severity of the injury. In most cases, rest, ice, compression, and elevation (RICE) is recommended to help reduce pain and swelling. Physical therapy may also be recommended to help improve strength and flexibility in the affected area. In severe cases, surgery may be necessary.

Fractures: A fracture refers to a break or crack in a bone. This type of injury can occur due to trauma, such as a fall or sports accident. Symptoms of a fracture may include pain, swelling, bruising, and difficulty moving the affected area.

Treatment: Treatment for fractures will depend on the location and severity of the injury. For minor fractures, immobilization with a cast or splint may be sufficient for healing. In more complex cases, surgery may be necessary to realign and stabilize the broken bone.

Lacerations: A laceration is a deep cut or tear in the skin. Treatment may include cleaning the wound, applying stitches or sutures if needed, and dressing the wound to promote healing.

At Kaysville Clinic, our injuries services aim to provide timely and effective treatment for a wide range of common injuries. Our experienced team is dedicated to helping you recover from your injury and get back to your daily activities as soon as possible.

What is Hormone Replacement Therapy
(HRT) for Menopause?
Menopause is the permanent cessation of the menstrual cycle caused by
the natural decline of ovarian function. This transition typically occurs
between the ages of 45 and 55, though some women may experience
delayed or early menopause. It marks the end of fertility and is
accompanied by hormonal fluctuations that can trigger a variety of
menopausal symptoms.
Common Symptoms
The symptoms of menopause can vary widely in intensity and impact.
Common issues include:
 Hot flashes and night sweats
 Mood swings and irritability
 Vaginal dryness and painful intercourse
 Sleep disturbances
 Fatigue
If left untreated, these symptoms can significantly affect a woman’s
quality of life.

Aesthetics

Skin-focused services designed to help you look and feel your best.

ClearLift, also known as the laser facelift, is a non-invasive and pain-free laser treatment that uses advanced technology to target signs of aging on the skin.

It works by creating controlled micro-injuries in deeper skin layers, stimulating collagen production and promoting cell turnover for a more youthful appearance.

Common treatment goals include reducing fine lines, tightening sagging skin, improving texture and tone, minimizing pores, and treating sun damage and acne scars.

Most sessions last 15-30 minutes with little to no downtime. Some mild redness may occur but usually subsides within a few hours.

Dye VL by Harmony XL Pro is an advanced laser treatment for pigmented lesions with precision and minimal discomfort.

It uses high-intensity light to break down excess melanin and can treat sunspots, age spots, freckles, melasma, and birthmarks.

Patients may feel a mild snapping sensation. Mild redness or swelling can occur temporarily, and visible improvement is often seen within a week.

At Kaysville Clinic, we offer Dye VL as a safe and effective option for all skin types to help improve clarity and skin tone.

Laser hair removal with Harmony XL Pro SHR uses low fluence pulses at a high repetition rate to gradually heat hair follicles while minimizing damage to surrounding tissue.

Benefits include long-lasting results, comfortable treatments, safety across skin types, quicker sessions, and no downtime.

After treatment, mild redness and sensitivity may occur briefly. Avoid sun exposure, hot showers, and excessive sweating for a few days.

Our team creates personalized treatment plans to help you achieve smooth, long-lasting hair reduction results.

Botox, short for Botulinum toxin, is used cosmetically to reduce facial wrinkles and lines, and medically for conditions such as chronic migraines, excessive sweating, overactive bladder, and muscle stiffness.

Botox works by blocking nerve signals to muscles, causing temporary relaxation that softens lines and can relieve selected medical symptoms.

It is a minimally invasive treatment with quick recovery and results that typically appear within days.

Sclerotherapy is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat varicose veins and spider veins by injecting a sclerosant solution into affected veins.

The treated vein collapses and is gradually absorbed by the body, reducing visible vein appearance and often improving discomfort.

Sessions are typically quick, involve minimal downtime, and many patients see significant cosmetic and symptomatic improvement.

What is Laser Hair Removal?
Laser hair removal is a cosmetic treatment that uses highly concentrated
laser light to selectively target and destroy hair follicles. The Harmony XL
Pro SHR device utilizes a special technology called Super Hair Removal
(SHR) which delivers low fluence pulses at a high repetition rate, gradually
heating the hair follicle while avoiding damage to surrounding tissue. This
results in effective and painless hair removal.
